What is Emotional Intelligence

The term Emotional Intelligence was first coined by two researchers, Salavoy and Mayer, in an article they published in 1990 in the journal Imagination, Cognition, and Personality. It was later further popularized in the 1995 book Emotional Intelligence by Dan Goleman. Emotional Intelligence is the ability to perceive, use, manage and handle emotions. This includes recognizing emotions in yourself and in others and then using that to guide your thinking and behavior and to adjust to your environment. The 4 domains of emotional intelligence are

  1. Self Awareness
  2. Self Management
  3. Social Awareness
  4. Relationship Management

EQ vs IQ

IQ, or intelligence quotient, is measures your ability to use logic, grasp complex ideas and solve problems. High IQ is important for:

  • Logical Reasoning
  • Working Memory
  • Analyzing complex situations
  • Research and Development
  • Word comprehension
  • Spatial thinking
EQ, or emotional quotient, as stated above, is the ability to identify, evaluate, and express emotions in yourself and perceive and assess emotions in others. High EQ is important for:
  • Teamwork
  • Leadership
  • Successful relationships
  • Collaboration
  • Understanding group dynamics

What should EQ matter to you?

Emotional intelligence affects your performance at work and in school, your physical and mental health as well as your relationship with others. High EQ helps to naviagte social complexities, form healthy relationships, reduce team stress, set appropriate boundaries, defuse conflict, manage stress, and helps you to lead and motivate others.
